# North America popular food

## Canada
  1. Poutine: A Canadian dish made with french fries, cheese curds, and gravy. Variations may include additional toppings like bacon, pulled pork, or mushrooms.
  2. Nanaimo Bars: A dessert consisting of a crumb base, custard or buttercream filling, and a layer of chocolate on top.
  3. Butter Tarts: Sweet pastries with a rich filling made from butter, sugar, and eggs, often with additions like raisins or pecans.

  5. Montreal-style Bagels: Dense and chewy bagels with a sweet and savory flavor, typically baked in a wood-fired oven and often topped with sesame seeds.

## Mexico
  1. Tacos: Corn or flour tortillas filled with various ingredients like grilled meat (such as carne asada or al pastor), fish, shrimp, or vegetables, typically accompanied by salsa, guacamole, and other toppings.
  2. Enchiladas: Tortillas rolled around a filling (such as shredded chicken, cheese, or beans) and topped with chili sauce and cheese, often served with rice and beans.
  3. Guacamole: A popular dip or topping made from mashed avocado, lime juice, onions, cilantro, and other seasonings.
  4. Chiles Rellenos: Whole peppers (usually poblano) stuffed with cheese or meat, battered, and fried, served with sauce or salsa.
  5. Tamales: Steamed or boiled corn dough filled with various fillings (such as pork, chicken, cheese, or sweet fillings), often wrapped in corn husks or banana leaves.

## United States
  1. Hamburger: A grilled or fried ground beef patty served in a bun, often with various toppings like cheese, lettuce, tomato, pickles, and condiments.
  2. Barbecue Ribs: Slow-cooked ribs (usually pork) coated with barbecue sauce, often served with coleslaw and cornbread.
  3. Buffalo Wings: Chicken wings deep-fried and coated in a spicy sauce, typically served with celery sticks and blue cheese or ranch dressing.
  4. Apple Pie: A classic American dessert made with a sweet apple filling and a flaky pastry crust.
  5. New York-style Pizza: Large, thin-crust pizza slices topped with tomato sauce, cheese, and various toppings.

These are just a few examples of popular foods in Canada, Mexico, and the United States. Each country has a wide range of regional dishes and culinary traditions to explore.
